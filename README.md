# The Honesty Matrix

*by Norbert Nopper*

A stipulative two-dimensional typology of truthful and untruthful utterances.

## Overview

The Honesty Matrix is a compact $2 \times 2$ typology that classifies
declarative utterances along two independent axes: (i) the *truth-value* of
the utterance with respect to the actual state of affairs, and (ii) the
speaker's *awareness* of the full epistemic and consequential status of that
utterance at the moment of speaking.

|           | Consciously                   | Unconsciously                    |
|-----------|-------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Truth** | Sincere truth-telling         | Unintended truth                 |
| **Lie**   | Deliberate lie                | Unwitting falsehood              |

The model is presented as a *stipulative* framework, not as a redefinition of
the philosophical notion of lying. It is intended to organise familiar
phenomena — sincere truth-telling, deliberate deception, the Freudian slip,
the white lie, the honest mistake, and Frankfurtian bullshit — inside a
single, explicit structure. The paper gives precise working definitions,
places the framework relative to existing accounts in the philosophy of
lying and psychoanalysis, works each cell through with examples, sketches a
minimal operationalisation, and states the model's limitations.
