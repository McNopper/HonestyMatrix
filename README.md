# Honesty Matrix

*by Norbert Nopper*

### When you tell the truth, do you know or believe it?

Epistemology studies the nature of knowledge and belief — in particular,
what distinguishes *knowing* something from merely *believing* it. The
**Honesty Matrix** adds a second, orthogonal dimension: whether the
speaker is *conscious* or *unconscious* of the truth or untruth being
told.

|           | Consciously                   | Unconsciously                    |
|-----------|-------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Truth** | Sincere truth-telling         | Unintended truth                 |
| **Lie**   | Deliberate lie                | Unwitting falsehood              |

Familiar phenomena map onto these cells: a Freudian slip that happens to
state a fact is an *unintended truth*; an honest mistake or a case of
self-deception is an *unwitting falsehood*; the *white lie* sits as a
boundary case between deliberate lie and unwitting falsehood; and
Frankfurtian "bullshit" is explicitly out of scope (indifference to
truth, not unawareness of it).

The full treatment — definitions, mapping, worked examples per cell,
operationalisation, limitations, and references — is in the paper.
